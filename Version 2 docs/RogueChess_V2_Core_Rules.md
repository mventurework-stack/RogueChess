# Rogue Chess V2 -- Core Game Rules

> **Design Goals**
>
> Rogue Chess is a highly strategic turn-based tactics game inspired by
> the depth of chess, but with its own futuristic identity. Every game
> should offer multiple viable strategies through army composition,
> positioning, and temporary card effects rather than permanent upgrades
> or random luck.
>
> **Core Principles**
>
> -   Positioning wins games.
> -   Army composition matters.
> -   Cards create opportunities, not victories.
> -   Every unit has strengths and weaknesses.
> -   There is no "best" army.
> -   Every unit type should be a viable choice.
> -   Temporary buffs only. No permanent unit upgrades.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Victory Condition

Each player has exactly one **Commander**.

If your Commander is destroyed, you immediately lose the game.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Starting Army

Every player starts with exactly **8 units**.

-   1 Commander (required)
-   7 additional units chosen freely

There are **no restrictions** on the remaining seven units.

Examples:

-   Commander + 7 Tanks
-   Commander + 7 Shooters
-   Commander + 7 Hackers
-   Commander + 7 Buddies
-   Commander + any combination

The goal is to balance the units so every army composition has a viable
strategy.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Turn Structure

Each turn:

-   One unit may perform one action.
-   One card may be played.
-   End Turn.

Unit actions include:

-   Move
-   Attack
-   Special ability (if applicable)

A unit may not perform multiple actions unless a temporary card
specifically allows it.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Cards

Cards function exactly as they do in Version 1.

Cards provide **temporary tactical effects only**.

Examples:

-   Extra movement
-   Temporary shield
-   Repair
-   Additional attack
-   Summon unit
-   Resource generation

Cards never permanently increase a unit's statistics.

There are:

-   No experience levels
-   No permanent upgrades
-   No evolving units

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Scrap

Scrap remains the game's primary resource.

Controlling Scrap Tiles and managing Scrap efficiently are important
strategic elements.

Players must constantly decide between:

-   Expanding
-   Defending
-   Fighting
-   Controlling resources

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Unit Roster

## Commander

-   Role: Leader
-   Movement: 1
-   Attack: Adjacent
-   Damage: 1
-   Health: 6

Special: - Losing the Commander loses the game.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Buddy

-   Role: Fast scout
-   Movement: 2
-   Attack: Adjacent
-   Damage: 1
-   Health: 3

Purpose: - Capture Scrap Tiles - Flank enemies - Pressure Shooters -
Block movement - Support attacks

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Shooter

-   Role: Long-range fire support
-   Movement: 1
-   Attack Range: 3
-   Damage: 1
-   Health: 2

Special: - Fires in a straight line. - Cannot shoot through units.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Tank

-   Role: Frontline defense
-   Movement: 1
-   Attack: Adjacent
-   Damage: 1
-   Health: 5

Purpose: - Hold territory - Protect the Commander - Protect Shooters

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Hacker

-   Role: Electronic warfare
-   Movement: 2
-   Attack: Adjacent
-   Damage: 1
-   Health: 2

Special Ability:

Instead of attacking, the Hacker may disable one adjacent enemy.

A disabled enemy:

-   Cannot act during its next turn.
-   May still be attacked.
-   Automatically recovers after missing one action.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Balance Philosophy

The game is **not balanced by limiting army composition**.

Instead, balance comes from giving every unit natural strengths and
weaknesses.

Players are free to experiment with any army they choose.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Strategic Relationships

-   Tank is strong against Buddy swarms.
-   Buddy is strong against Shooters through mobility.
-   Shooter is strong against slow Tank formations.
-   Hacker is strong against defensive formations by disrupting key
    units.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Army Archetypes

-   Fortress
-   Mobile Strike
-   Fire Support
-   Electronic Warfare
-   Balanced Task Force

Every archetype should be capable of winning when played well.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Design Philosophy

Victory should come from:

-   Better positioning
-   Better timing
-   Better card usage
-   Better resource management
-   Better tactical decisions

No single army composition should dominate the others.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Future Expansion

Future units should:

-   Have a unique battlefield role.
-   Have clear strengths and weaknesses.
-   Create new strategies instead of replacing existing units.
-   Preserve the game's strategic depth without adding unnecessary
    complexity.
