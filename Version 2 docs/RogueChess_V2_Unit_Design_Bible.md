# Rogue Chess V2 -- Unit Design Bible

## Purpose

This document defines the rules for creating and balancing every unit in
Rogue Chess.

The objective is to ensure every unit is useful, every army composition
is viable, and strategy---not restrictions---determines the winner.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Core Philosophy

-   No unit is strictly better than another.
-   Every unit has a battlefield role.
-   Every strength must have a meaningful weakness.
-   Players may choose any legal army composition.
-   Balance comes from counters, positioning, cards, and player
    decisions.
-   Cards provide temporary effects only. Units never receive permanent
    upgrades.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Design Pillars

## 1. Every Unit Has One Primary Role

Examples:

-   Leader
-   Frontline
-   Mobility
-   Ranged Fire
-   Battlefield Control
-   Support

Avoid creating units that excel at multiple roles simultaneously.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 2. Strength Requires Weakness

Examples:

A durable Tank sacrifices speed.

A Shooter sacrifices durability.

A Buddy sacrifices toughness for mobility.

A Hacker sacrifices survivability for tactical control.

Every advantage must be paid for somewhere else.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 3. No Auto-Include Units

A player should never think:

"I must always take this unit."

Every unit should instead support a particular strategy.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 4. No Dead Units

A unit should never exist that experienced players ignore.

If a unit is rarely selected, improve its role rather than restricting
other units.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 5. No Hard Counters

Units should create favorable matchups, not automatic victories.

Good positioning and card play should always matter.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Current Units

## Commander

Role: Leader

Purpose: Primary victory condition.

Should never become the strongest combat unit.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Buddy

Role: Mobility

Purpose:

-   Reach Scrap first.
-   Flank.
-   Screen allies.
-   Chase ranged units.

Primary strength: Speed.

Primary weakness: Moderate durability.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Shooter

Role: Fire Support

Purpose:

-   Area denial.
-   Long-range pressure.
-   Defensive support.

Primary strength: Range.

Primary weakness: Fragility.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Tank

Role: Frontline

Purpose:

-   Hold territory.
-   Protect allies.
-   Block movement.

Primary strength: Durability.

Primary weakness: Low mobility.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Hacker

Role: Battlefield Control

Purpose:

-   Disable key enemy units.
-   Break defensive formations.
-   Create tactical openings.

Primary strength: Control.

Primary weakness: Fragility.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Balance Guidelines

When designing a new unit, answer these questions:

1.  What unique role does it fill?
2.  What existing unit does it complement?
3.  What existing unit naturally pressures it?
4.  What weakness prevents it from dominating?
5.  Would a player reasonably choose seven copies of it?

If the answer to question 5 is "never", redesign the unit.

If the answer is "always", weaken the unit.

The ideal answer is:

"Sometimes, depending on strategy."

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Army Diversity

Every pure army should have a viable plan.

Examples:

-   Commander + 7 Buddies
-   Commander + 7 Shooters
-   Commander + 7 Tanks
-   Commander + 7 Hackers

Mixed armies should gain flexibility rather than raw power.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Power Budget

Increasing one attribute should generally require reducing another.

Examples:

Higher health → lower mobility.

Higher mobility → lower durability.

Powerful control → lower survivability.

Longer range → weaker close combat.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Future Units

Future units should expand strategy rather than complexity.

They should:

-   Introduce a new tactical role.
-   Create new army archetypes.
-   Preserve simple rules.
-   Interact cleanly with existing cards.

Avoid units that rely on exceptions or complicated rule text.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Success Criteria

A balanced Rogue Chess roster allows:

-   Multiple viable army compositions.
-   Multiple opening strategies.
-   Different playstyles.
-   Decisions driven by positioning, timing, Scrap, and temporary card
    effects.

If one army consistently dominates equally skilled opponents, adjust
unit statistics rather than restricting player choice.
